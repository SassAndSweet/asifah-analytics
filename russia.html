🏛️ Government & Political System
▼

Russia is a consolidated authoritarian system centered on President Vladimir Putin, who has held power as either president or prime minister since 1999 — making him the longest-serving Russian/Soviet leader since Stalin.

  • Putin's Consolidation: 2020 constitutional amendments allow rule to 2036. March 2024 election: 87% officially reported, no genuine opposition permitted.
  • Silovik Power Base: The FSB (domestic), SVR (foreign), and GRU (military intelligence) wield enormous power. The "siloviki" — security and military establishment — form Putin's core power base.
  • Succession Risk: Putin (age 72) has no public succession plan. Lack of institutional mechanisms for orderly power transfer is widely considered Russia's most significant long-term stability risk.
  • Prigozhin Mutiny (June 2023): Wagner Group leader's armed march on Moscow — the most serious challenge to Kremlin authority in Putin's tenure. Prigozhin killed in plane crash two months later.
Based on Carnegie Russia Eurasia Center, Levada Center, IISS, and Chatham House reporting

☢️ Military & Nuclear Posture
▼

Russia maintains the world's largest nuclear arsenal (~5,580 warheads) and the second-largest conventional military, though the Ukraine war has exposed significant weaknesses in doctrine, equipment, and logistics.

  • Nuclear Doctrine (2024 Revision): Lowered threshold for nuclear use — aggression by a non-nuclear state supported by a nuclear power could trigger nuclear response. Western analysts view this as deliberate coercive ambiguity.
  • Ukraine Losses: Over 300,000 casualties (KIA + WIA); thousands of tanks, armored vehicles, aircraft destroyed. Pre-war force structure fundamentally degraded.
  • DPRK & Iranian Support: North Korea providing millions of artillery shells and reportedly troops. Iran supplying Shahed drones. Both relationships have deepened into broader military-technical cooperation.
  • Defense Production: Russia shifted to wartime footing — increased shell, tank, and drone manufacturing. Dependent on imported microelectronics — subject to Western export controls.
Based on IISS, SIPRI, RUSI, Janes, and CSIS reporting
📑 IISS 📑 SIPRI 📑 RUSI 📑 Arms Control
💰 Sanctions & Economic Impact
▼

Russia faces the most comprehensive sanctions regime ever imposed on a major economy, targeting financial institutions, energy exports, and technology imports. The impact is significant but Russia has demonstrated greater short-term resilience than initially expected.

  • ~$300B Frozen: Russian Central Bank reserves frozen in Western jurisdictions. Major banks SWIFT-disconnected.
  • Energy Redirect: Oil and gas exports redirected to China, India, Turkey at discounted prices. Revenue declined but not collapsed.
  • Shadow Fleet: Russia assembled aging oil tanker fleet to circumvent shipping restrictions — significant environmental risk in European and Arctic waters.
  • Wartime Economy: Defense spending exceeding 6-8% of GDP. Short-term GDP growth driven by military production — at cost of civilian investment and accelerating inflation.
Based on EU sanctions database, OFAC/Treasury, IMF, World Bank, and KSE Institute tracking

✊ Internal Dissent & Civil Society
▼

Russia's domestic political space has been dramatically constricted since the Ukraine invasion. Independent media, opposition movements, and civil society have been systematically eliminated, driven underground, or forced into exile.

  • Navalny: Died in Arctic penal colony February 2024. Removed the most prominent remaining domestic opposition figure.
  • Media Suppression: Novaya Gazeta, Meduza, TV Rain forced to operate from exile. Criticizing the war carries up to 15 years imprisonment.
  • Exile Opposition: Significant Russian opposition community in Lithuania, Georgia, Germany. Reach inside Russia limited by information control — millions access via VPN.
Based on OVD-Info, RSF, Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, Memorial

🎯 Ukraine War Strategy & Objectives
▼

Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine (February 24, 2022) is the largest conventional military conflict in Europe since 1945. Russia holds approximately 18% of Ukrainian territory including Crimea.

  • Stated Objectives: Evolved from "denazification" to control of four illegally annexed oblasts (Donetsk, Luhansk, Zaporizhzhia, Kherson) and preventing NATO membership.
  • ICC Warrants: Putin and Children's Rights Commissioner Maria Lvova-Belova — unlawful deportation of Ukrainian children, March 2023.
  • Ceasefire Prospects: Trump-Putin talks, Chinese framework, and third-party mediations all floated. Key obstacles: Ukrainian territorial integrity, Russian occupation, security guarantees.
Based on ISW, RUSI, IISS, UN Human Rights Monitoring Mission, and ICC reporting
📑 ISW 📑 ICC 📑 Crisis Group
🌐 Russia-China & Global Alignment
▼

The Russia-China relationship has deepened significantly since the invasion, evolving from a "partnership without limits" into Russia's most critical international relationship as Western isolation intensified.

  • Economic Dependence: Bilateral trade exceeding $240 billion in 2024. Russia dependent on Chinese imports for consumer goods, electronics, and industrial equipment.
  • Technology Transfer: China provides dual-use technologies — components appearing in Russian weapons systems. Western intelligence documented Chinese components in Russian drones and precision munitions.
  • BRICS & Alternative Order: Russia invested in expanded BRICS, de-dollarization, and alternative payment systems as strategy to circumvent Western isolation.
Based on SIPRI, Brookings, Carnegie, IISS, and academic research
