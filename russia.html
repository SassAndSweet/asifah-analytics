🏛️ Government & Political System
▼

Russia is a consolidated authoritarian system centered on President Vladimir Putin, who has held power since 1999 — the longest-serving Russian/Soviet leader since Stalin.

  • Putin's Consolidation: Constitutional amendments (2020) allow Putin to remain in office until 2036. March 2024 election returned Putin with officially reported 87%. Independent observers: neither free nor fair.
  • State Duma: 450-seat lower house dominated by United Russia. Functions as a legislative rubber stamp. Genuine opposition excluded or suppressed.
  • Siloviki: FSB (domestic security), SVR (foreign intelligence), GRU (military intelligence) wield enormous power. Security establishment forms Putin's core power base.
  • Succession Question: Putin (age 72) has no public succession plan. Historical Russian/Soviet leadership transitions have frequently been chaotic.
  • Prigozhin Mutiny (June 2023): Wagner Group leader's brief armed march on Moscow — the most serious challenge to Kremlin authority in Putin's tenure. Prigozhin killed in a plane crash two months later.
* Based on Carnegie Russia Eurasia Center, Levada Center, IISS, and Chatham House reporting

⚔️ Military & Nuclear Posture
▼

Russia maintains the world's largest nuclear arsenal and the second-largest conventional military, though the Ukraine war has exposed significant weaknesses in doctrine, equipment, and logistics.

  • Nuclear Arsenal: ~5,580 nuclear warheads — world's largest stockpile. Strategic triad: ICBMs (Topol-M, Yars), submarine-launched (Bulava on Borei-class SSBNs), and air-launched cruise missiles.
  • Nuclear Doctrine (2024): Revised doctrine lowered threshold for nuclear use — aggression by a non-nuclear state supported by a nuclear power could trigger nuclear response. Analysts: deliberate ambiguity to deter Western involvement.
  • Ukraine Losses: 300,000+ casualties (KIA + WIA), thousands of tanks and armored vehicles. Pre-war force structure fundamentally degraded.
  • Mobilization: Sept 2022 partial mobilization: ~300,000 reservists. Now relies on high-salary contracts, prison recruitment, and foreign fighters (Nepal, Central Africa, DPRK).
  • DPRK & Iranian Support: North Korea: millions of artillery shells, reportedly deployed troops. Iran: Shahed-series drones. China: dual-use components confirmed (NYT, Apr 2026).
* Based on IISS, SIPRI, RUSI, Janes, and CSIS reporting

💰 Sanctions & Economic Impact
▼

Russia faces the most comprehensive sanctions regime ever imposed on a major economy — targeting financial institutions, energy exports, technology imports, and individuals. Russia has shown greater short-term resilience than initially expected, but long-term structural damage is severe.

  • Financial Sanctions: ~$300B in Central Bank reserves frozen. Major banks disconnected from SWIFT. Ruble stabilized through capital controls and energy revenues.
  • Energy Revenues: Oil and gas historically ~40% of federal budget. G7 price cap ($60/bbl). Russia redirected exports to China, India, Turkey at discounted prices via shadow fleet.
  • Technology Restrictions: Export controls on advanced semiconductors and dual-use technologies. Sanctions evasion through intermediary countries continues. Chinese components in Russian weapons documented.
  • Wartime Economy: Defense spending 6–8% of GDP. Short-term GDP growth via military production but at cost of civilian investment, labor distortions, and 21% CBR key rate.
  • Shadow Fleet: Russia assembled a fleet of aging oil tankers to circumvent Western shipping restrictions — raising significant environmental and insurance concerns in European and Arctic waters.
* Based on EU sanctions database, OFAC/Treasury, IMF, World Bank, and KSE Institute tracking

✊ Internal Dissent & Civil Society
▼

Russia's domestic political space has been dramatically constricted since the Ukraine invasion. Independent media, opposition movements, and civil society have been systematically eliminated or forced into exile.

  • Navalny (Feb 2024): Alexei Navalny died in IK-6 Arctic penal colony under circumstances widely attributed to state action. Removed the most prominent remaining domestic opposition figure.
  • Media Suppression: Novaya Gazeta, Meduza, TV Rain shut down or operating from exile. Criticizing the war: criminal offense, up to 15 years imprisonment.
  • Anti-War Movement: 16,000+ detained in Feb–Mar 2022 protests. Effectively suppressed. Organized domestic opposition now almost entirely covert.
  • Ethnic Minorities: Casualty rates disproportionately borne by minority regions (Dagestan, Buryatia). Generated localized resentment but not yet organized resistance.
  • Exile Opposition: Russian opposition operates from abroad (Lithuania, Georgia, Germany, Israel). Exile media reaches millions of Russians through VPNs.
* Based on Memorial, OVD-Info, RSF, Amnesty International, and Human Rights Watch

🎯 Ukraine War Strategy & Objectives
▼

Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine (February 24, 2022) is the largest conventional military conflict in Europe since 1945 and has fundamentally altered the European security architecture and global energy markets.

  • Stated Objectives: Evolved from "denazification and demilitarization" to effective control of four Ukrainian oblasts (Donetsk, Luhansk, Zaporizhzhia, Kherson, illegally annexed Sept 2022) and preventing Ukraine's NATO membership.
  • Military Situation: After initial failures at Kyiv, a grinding attritional war concentrated in eastern and southern Ukraine. Russia holds ~18% of Ukrainian territory (including Crimea).
  • Escalation Dynamics: Russia has deployed Iranian-designed Shahed drones, North Korean ammunition, and sophisticated strike packages against Ukrainian infrastructure — sustained campaigns against the energy grid, civilian infrastructure, and port facilities.
  • Ceasefire Prospects: Multiple proposals floated (Trump "peace plan," Chinese framework, third-party mediations). Key obstacles: Ukraine's territorial integrity, Russia's refusal to withdraw, security guarantees, sanctions relief question.
  • War Crimes & ICC: ICC issued arrest warrants for Putin and Lvova-Belova in March 2023 for unlawful deportation of Ukrainian children. Documented atrocities at Bucha, Irpin, and Mariupol extensively investigated.
* Based on ISW, RUSI, IISS, UN Human Rights Monitoring Mission, and ICC reporting

🌐 Russia-China & Global Alignment
▼

The Russia-China relationship has deepened significantly since the Ukraine invasion, evolving from a "partnership without limits" (declared weeks before the invasion) into Russia's most critical international relationship.

  • Economic Dependence: Bilateral trade exceeded $240B in 2024. Russia depends heavily on Chinese imports for consumer goods, electronics, automobiles, and industrial equipment that sanctions cut off.
  • Energy Corridor: Russia redirected oil and gas exports toward China via Power of Siberia pipeline. China benefits from discounted Russian energy. Power of Siberia 2 under negotiation.
  • Technology & Military (2026): US intelligence (NYT, Apr 2026) reports China may have shipped MANPADS to Iran; providing dual-use chemicals, fuel, and components to Russia. Russia providing satellite targeting intelligence to IRGC. China officially denies lethal military aid.
  • Strategic Coordination: Joint military exercises, coordinated UN vetoes, aligned positions on international institutions, and shared opposition to "U.S. hegemony." Falls short of formal alliance.
  • BRICS & Alternative Order: Russia invested heavily in BRICS (expanded 2024: Iran, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Egypt, Ethiopia) as alternative to Western-led institutions. De-dollarization and South-South diplomacy central to strategy.
* Based on SIPRI, Brookings, Carnegie, IISS research. NYT reporting April 2026.
