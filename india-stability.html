⛰️ India-China Border & LAC
▼

The Line of Actual Control (LAC) is the de facto border between India and China along a 3,488 km frontier in the Himalayas. It has never been formally delineated, creating persistent friction.

Key Developments:

  • Galwan Valley (2020): Deadliest clash in 45 years; 20 Indian and 4+ Chinese soldiers killed in hand-to-hand combat
  • Disengagement: Partial pullbacks at several friction points (Gogra, Hot Springs, Pangong Tso) following diplomatic talks
  • Infrastructure Race: Both sides conducting massive border road, airfield, and facility construction programs
  • Depsang Plains: Chinese incursions into historically Indian-patrolled territory; limited disengagement progress
  • Strategic Implications: India has pivoted toward QUAD alignment and U.S. defense cooperation in response to Chinese aggression

🏔️ Kashmir & Pakistan Tensions
▼

Kashmir remains the central dispute between nuclear-armed India and Pakistan, with both countries claiming the territory in full while controlling separate portions.

Current Situation:

  • Article 370: India revoked Jammu & Kashmir's special status in August 2019, bifurcating it into two Union Territories; Pakistan condemned the move
  • Line of Control: Periodic ceasefire violations despite 2021 agreement; Indian and Pakistani forces maintain heavy forward deployments
  • Militancy: Pakistan-based groups (Lashkar-e-Taiba, Jaish-e-Mohammed) conduct cross-border attacks; Pahalgam attack (2025) killed 26 tourists
  • Nuclear Dimension: Both India and Pakistan possess nuclear weapons; escalation risks are real
  • Diplomatic: Bilateral relations effectively frozen since 2019; no scheduled summits

🏛️ Internal Security & Political Dynamics
▼

India faces a range of internal security challenges even as it cements its position as the world's most populous country and fifth-largest economy.

Key Issues:

  • Naxalism: Maoist insurgency (Naxalites) in central India (Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand); declining but persistent; India's longest-running insurgency
  • Northeast: Various ethnic insurgencies in Manipur, Nagaland, and Assam; Manipur communal violence (2023) displaced hundreds of thousands
  • Communal Tensions: Hindu-Muslim tensions elevated under BJP governance; Citizenship Amendment Act protests; periodic riots
  • Press Freedom: India ranked 161/180 in RSF Press Freedom Index; foreign journalists increasingly restricted
  • Democracy: Concerns about democratic backsliding; opposition leaders face legal challenges; electoral bond controversy

🌊 Indo-Pacific Strategy & QUAD
▼

India occupies a pivotal position in the Indo-Pacific security architecture, balancing strategic autonomy with deepening U.S. partnership.

Strategic Dynamics:

  • QUAD: India, U.S., Japan, Australia; increasing military exercises (Malabar); intelligence sharing; technology cooperation
  • Defense Cooperation: India is now a major U.S. arms purchaser (Apache helicopters, P-8 Poseidon); BECA, LEMOA, COMCASA signed
  • Russia Relations: India maintains ties with Russia (oil imports, S-400 purchase); balances Western pressure with strategic autonomy
  • Indian Ocean: India considers the Indian Ocean its sphere of influence; countering Chinese port investments (Hambantota, Gwadar)
  • SCO & BRICS: India is member of both Russia/China-led forums while deepening Western alignment
