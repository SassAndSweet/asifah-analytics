💣 TTP & Internal Insurgency
▼

Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan (TTP), or the Pakistani Taliban, has dramatically escalated attacks since its 2022 ceasefire breakdown, constituting Pakistan's gravest internal security threat.

Operational Profile:

  • Sanctuary: TTP leadership and fighters operate from Afghan territory under Taliban protection; Pakistan's demands for crackdown have been rebuffed
  • Attack Tempo: 2023-2024 saw the highest attack rates since the 2014 Army Public School massacre; security forces bear the brunt of casualties
  • Geographic Reach: TTP operates in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, FATA, Balochistan, and has conducted high-profile attacks in Karachi and Lahore
  • Pakistan Airstrikes: Pakistan has conducted multiple cross-border airstrikes into Afghanistan targeting TTP positions, raising bilateral tensions
  • Splinter Groups: TTP is an umbrella; Hafiz Gul Bahadur Group and others operate semi-independently

🗺️ Iran Border & Regional Tensions
▼

The Pakistan-Iran border emerged as a major flashpoint in January 2024 when both countries exchanged missile and drone strikes — a rare direct confrontation between two nominally non-hostile states.

Key Dynamics:

  • January 2024 Strikes: Iran fired missiles into Balochistan targeting Jaish al-Adl (Sunni militant group); Pakistan retaliated with strikes inside Iran's Sistan-Baluchestan province
  • Jaish al-Adl: Sunni Baloch militant group operates from Pakistani territory, conducting attacks on Iranian security forces; designated terrorist organization by both countries
  • BLA: Balochistan Liberation Army conducts attacks on Chinese interests (CPEC projects) and Pakistani security forces; some Iranian sympathy for Baloch grievances
  • CPEC: China-Pakistan Economic Corridor projects (Gwadar port, roads, energy) are targets for both TTP and BLA attacks
  • Drug Trafficking: Major narcotics transit route; fuels militant financing

⚔️ India-Pakistan Rivalry & Nuclear
▼

India and Pakistan have fought four wars and remain locked in one of the world's most dangerous nuclear rivalries, with Kashmir at the center of their dispute.

Current State:

  • 2025 Escalation: Following the Pahalgam tourist attack in Indian-administered Kashmir (26 killed), India launched Operation Sindoor — strikes on Pakistani territory targeting militant infrastructure; most significant cross-border escalation in decades
  • Nuclear Posture: Pakistan has ~170 warheads; Pakistan's nuclear doctrine emphasizes first use against conventional Indian military advance
  • Army Dominance: Pakistan Army remains the dominant political institution; civilian governments operate within military-defined limits
  • IMF Dependence: Pakistan has required multiple IMF bailouts; economic fragility limits strategic options
  • China Alignment: Pakistan-China "iron brotherhood" includes CPEC, arms transfers (JF-17 jets, Type 054A frigates), and diplomatic support at UNSC

🏛️ Political Instability & Governance
▼

Pakistan's political system has been characterized by chronic instability, with repeated cycles of military rule, civilian governments, and judicial crises.

Current Situation:

  • Imran Khan: Former PM imprisoned since August 2023; his PTI party remains the most popular political force despite systematic suppression; mass protests
  • Shahbaz Sharif: Current PM; PML-N government backed by military establishment; contested 2024 elections gave PTI plurality but coalition excluded them
  • Judiciary: Constitutional courts used as political weapons; judges face pressure; rule of law concerns
  • Economy: Persistent current account deficits; near-default avoided through IMF program; inflation has exceeded 30% at peak
  • Internet/Media: X (Twitter) and VPNs blocked; journalists targeted; Pakistan ranks poorly on press freedom
