🏛️The Knesset & Coalition Politics
▼

Israel is a parliamentary democracy operating under proportional representation with a 3.25% electoral threshold. No party has ever won an outright Knesset majority, making coalition governments unavoidable.

The Current Coalition (formed Dec 2022): Comprises Netanyahu's Likud (32), Shas (11), United Torah Judaism (7), Religious Zionism (7), Otzma Yehudit (6), and Noam (1) — holding 64 of 120 seats. The coalition is structurally fragile: any single partner's withdrawal collapses the government.

Netanyahu's Criminal Trial: On trial for bribery, fraud, and breach of trust — charges he denies. The ongoing trial creates unique governance tensions with no parallel in Western democracies.

Judicial Reform Crisis (2023): Attempt to curtail Supreme Court powers triggered the largest protest movement in Israeli history — paused after October 7 but not resolved.

2026 Elections: Must be held by November 2026. Early elections possible if coalition fractures over war strategy, hostage deal terms, or Haredi conscription. Likud showing poll gains amid war.

⚔️IDF & Security Architecture
▼

The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) is the most capable military in the Middle East, sustained by universal conscription and an extensive trained reserve force mobilizable in 48–72 hours.

Active Fronts (2026): Simultaneous operations in Gaza, Lebanon (ceasefire Nov 2024), and direct strikes on Iranian infrastructure — the most complex multi-front operation since 1948. IDF has now launched 15+ waves of strikes against Iranian targets including Tehran and Isfahan infrastructure.

Intelligence Services: Shin Bet (domestic), Mossad (foreign), and Unit 8200 (signals). All three suffered historic failures ahead of October 7. Unit 8200 and 9900 credited with providing key intelligence for strikes on Khamenei's underground bunker.

Layered Missile Defense: Iron Dome, David's Sling, Arrow 2/3. Successfully intercepting Iranian missile barrages with continued U.S. support including THAAD batteries.

📉Economy, Society & War Costs
▼

War Economic Impact: Defense spending ~8–9% of GDP. Bank of Israel estimated total war costs exceeding $56B through 2025. Jerusalem's Mahane Yehuda market and small businesses reporting severe impacts from ongoing Iran war.

NIS/USD: The shekel has strengthened recently (currently ~3.09/USD, down from ~3.7+ at war onset), reflecting some market confidence but remaining highly sensitive to escalation news.

Credit Ratings: Moody's (A2), S&P, Fitch all downgraded Israel in 2024 — first downgrades in Israeli history.

Haredi Conscription: Supreme Court ruled the state cannot fund yeshiva students who refuse military service. Threatens coalition's Haredi partners and remains a live constitutional crisis.

Displacement: ~80,000 Israelis displaced from northern border communities; ~60,000 from Gaza-envelope communities.
