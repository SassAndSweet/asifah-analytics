⚔️ Iran-Backed Militias & PMF
▼

Iraq hosts powerful Iran-aligned militia groups operating under the Popular Mobilization Forces (PMF/Hashd al-Shaabi) umbrella. Key groups include Kata'ib Hezbollah, Asa'ib Ahl al-Haq, and Harakat al-Nujaba. These groups have repeatedly attacked U.S. forces at Al Asad, Erbil, and other bases.

Islamic Resistance in Iraq: This umbrella brand coordinates attacks on U.S. targets, using drones, rockets, and ballistic missiles. Operations have escalated dramatically during the current Iran conflict.

* Based on open-source intelligence, think tank analysis, and government publications

🎖️ U.S. Military Presence
▼

Approximately 2,500 U.S. troops remain in Iraq as part of the anti-ISIS coalition. Key installations include Al Asad Air Base (Anbar), Erbil (Kurdistan), and the Baghdad Embassy compound. These bases face frequent rocket and drone attacks from Iran-backed groups.

* Based on open-source intelligence, think tank analysis, and government publications

☠️ ISIS Remnants
▼

ISIS maintains a low-level insurgency in rural areas of central and northern Iraq, conducting assassinations, ambushes, and IED attacks. The group exploits governance vacuums and sectarian grievances. Kurdish Peshmerga and Iraqi Security Forces conduct ongoing counter-ISIS operations.

* Based on open-source intelligence, think tank analysis, and government publications

🏛️ Political Landscape
▼

Iraq's political system is characterized by sectarian power-sharing, Kurdish autonomy tensions, and significant Iranian influence. Prime Minister Mohammed Shia al-Sudani leads a government backed by the Iran-aligned Coordination Framework. Baghdad-Erbil relations over oil revenue sharing remain contentious.
