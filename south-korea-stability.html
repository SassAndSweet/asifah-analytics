🚀 North Korean Threat & Deterrence
▼

South Korea faces the most immediate North Korean threat of any nation, with Seoul's 25 million metropolitan residents within range of North Korean conventional artillery, rockets, and nuclear weapons.

Threat Assessment:

  • Artillery: North Korea has thousands of artillery pieces and rocket launchers within range of Seoul, capable of massive conventional destruction in the opening hours of any conflict
  • Nuclear: KN-23, KN-24, and KN-25 short-range ballistic missiles explicitly designed for Korean peninsula use; some assessed as nuclear-capable
  • Special Operations: 200,000+ DPRK SOF; tunnels under the DMZ; infiltration capability
  • Cyber: North Korea has conducted major cyberattacks on South Korean banks, government systems, and defense contractors
  • ROK Response: Korea Massive Punishment and Retaliation (KMPR) strategy threatens decapitation strikes on DPRK leadership in response to nuclear use

🤝 U.S.-ROK Alliance & USFK
▼

The U.S.-ROK Mutual Defense Treaty (1953) and the presence of 28,500 U.S. troops form the bedrock of South Korean security.

Alliance Dynamics:

  • USFK: 28,500 U.S. troops; Camp Humphreys (largest U.S. overseas base); 7th Air Force at Osan; 2nd Infantry Division
  • Combined Forces Command: U.S.-ROK integrated command; OPCON transfer debate ongoing (South Korea seeks wartime operational control)
  • Extended Deterrence: Washington Declaration (2023) established Nuclear Consultative Group; U.S. strategic assets rotating through peninsula
  • Japan Triangle: Camp David trilateral (2023) formalized U.S.-ROK-Japan security cooperation; intelligence sharing expanded
  • Burden Sharing: Host Nation Support agreements periodically renegotiated; ROK now among U.S.'s highest burden-sharing partners

🏛️ Domestic Political Situation
▼

South Korea's democracy experienced a major shock when President Yoon Suk-yeol declared martial law in December 2024, the first such declaration since 1980, before the National Assembly voted to overturn it within hours.

Key Developments:

  • Martial Law Crisis: Yoon declared martial law December 3, 2024; National Assembly emergency session voted 190-0 to lift it within 6 hours; Yoon complied
  • Impeachment: National Assembly impeached Yoon December 14, 2024; Constitutional Court upheld impeachment April 2025
  • Acting Presidents: Multiple acting presidents served during the constitutional crisis period
  • Snap Election: Presidential election held June 2025 following Constitutional Court ruling; Lee Jae-myung (Democratic Party) won
  • Alliance Impact: Crisis temporarily raised questions about U.S.-ROK alliance coordination; new government has reaffirmed alliance commitments

🌉 Inter-Korean Relations
▼

Inter-Korean relations are at a historic low, with North Korea declaring South Korea its primary enemy and severing all communication channels.

Current Situation:

  • Kaesong/Diamond Mountain: All inter-Korean economic projects suspended; North has demolished some joint facilities
  • Communication: All hotlines severed by North Korea; no regular diplomatic contact
  • Balloon Operations: Both sides conducting information operations via balloons; North sending trash balloons, South sending leaflets and USB drives
  • Border Fortification: North Korea mining and fortifying its side of the DMZ extensively
  • New Policy: Lee Jae-myung's government has signaled interest in re-engagement but North Korea has not responded to overtures
